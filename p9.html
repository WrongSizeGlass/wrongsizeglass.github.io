What is Geo City

It's a game where NPCs give different interactions to the player character.

It's a game filled with geometric objects and bad jokes.

Help the other geometric forms in the city.

Negative interactions should not be hurtful to the player but should be direct enough so the player knows that they are in a negative interaction.

Design values

The quest the NPCs give to the player should be simple enough, as the player should focus on the dialogue interactions

The exploration of intersectional biases through interactive dialogue, utilizing distinct dialogues for both NPCs and players based on appearance

Design pillars

The project aimed to see if the players could identify if they experienced intersectional biases based on the dialogue they encountered. The NPC and the player should have distinct dialogue depending on what the player and the active NPC look like, and the NPC should react to the player based on the NPC's current perception of the player. Negative interactions should not be hurtful to the player. Thus the characters should not be humanoid so that negative dialogue can be negative with the player might reflect the comments on themselves.

Reputation system

The image above shows an overview and simplification of the reputation system attributes with the self character's biases, as well as the goodwill the character has towards the other characters. These stats also affect what type of dialogue the other characters say to the self character. For the NPCs, the final value of the goodwill attribute will dictate how the NPC will respond to the player and if the player will receive a reward from the NPC.
The image above represents a more detailed visualization of how the reputation system works and which attributes are affected by other attributes. While this system has some flaws regarding intersectional inspiration, it serves as an early prototype for a more complex reputation system.
Based on the world-building and the reasoning for different biases, most NPCs generally have positive perceptions towards characters who belong to the following visual social groups: single-colored, thick, and triangular prisms. Conversely, the NPCs would generally have a negative perception towards characters who belong to the following visual social groups: checked, thin, and cubed. The image above shows all the rewards the player can receive if they only choose the polite dialogue option towards the quest-giving NPCs.

My role

As mentioned earlier, this was a solo project, so I handled all aspects myself. I was responsible for system design, quest design, level design, game design, QA testing, and programming.

I dedicated a significant amount of time to researching and developing the reputation system and systemic game design. I also conducted ideation workshops to generate interesting interactions.

The reputation system I developed is functional and can serve as a foundation for more dynamic and individual reputation systems in various game genres, such as RPGs. It can also be used to educate and raise awareness about intersectional biases, which was the original intent of the project.

During the design process, I created numerous interesting interactions, both positive and negative. If you are interested, I would be happy to assist you in creating intriguing reputational interactions for your own project.
