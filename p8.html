What is Your Friendly Neighborhood Raccoon

It's a game where you get to play as a Raccoon in a city.

You get to explore the city and jump on top of buildings.

Help fixing and cleaning the city

The problems need to be simple enough so the player can figure them out without having to tell them.

Design values

The solutions to the problems need to be solvable by movement and/or grabbing mechanics, resulting in the solution being intuitive to what they know they can do.

The lighthearted and chill game focuses on problem-solving in a city, exploring the difference between linear and open-quest approaches.

Design pillers

The project's main goal was to find out how people would solve problems in a game and in a linear vs open quest approach.
The player should not feel stress which could resolve in a high cognitive load other than solving the problems.
The problems can be weird, allowing for a weird comment about the problem to the player to gain a cheap grin from them since their motivation to participate in the test is important, as it is within the group test.

My roll

For this project, I had a quite fluid role spanning from Quest, Level and Game designer, QA tester, and Programmer.
Since I was responsible for:
designing and implementing the level layout, block out basic objects and their positions.
The second quest is in the park with both solutions, and the third quest at the power plant.
The gameplay mechanics, the sound effects, including Playtesting and fixing bugs
Lastly, I scripted the quest controllers, the savefile write/read json script, UI functionality and more.

Features 3D heatmap

To evaluate the players' movement, to see if they vent into problems, such as where the players fail jumps, the most common paths, etc. I developed a 3D heatmap that tracked the players' positions.
This heatmap was quite educational to analyse and has helped me with my later designs and given me good inputs on how to improve the level.
